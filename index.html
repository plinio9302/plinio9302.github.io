World Cup Champion Prediction — Monte Carlo Simulation

PythonElo ratingsPoisson modelsMonte CarloNumPy
Problem
Estimate each national team's probability of winning the World Cup — a problem with no single right answer, only well-calibrated uncertainty.
Data
Historical international match results used to fit team strength ratings.
Method
Three-layer pipeline built from scratch: Elo ratings for team strength → Poisson models for match scorelines → Monte Carlo simulation of the full tournament thousands of times.
Result
A fully annotated probabilistic model producing championship odds for every team, demonstrating statistical modeling beyond off-the-shelf libraries.
3-layer pipelineThousands of simulated tournamentsBuilt from scratch
In plain termsInstead of guessing who wins the World Cup, I simulated the whole tournament thousands of times to compute each team's real chances — the same technique used in forecasting and risk analysis.
